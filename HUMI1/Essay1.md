## Submission
Tsao, Calvin. "IM Pei: 1917–2019." arq: Architectural Research Quarterly 23.3 (2019): 204-209.  www.cambridge.org/core/services/aop-cambridge-core/content/view/F00B878899AABACD54EA6D4601097235/S135913551900037Xa.pdf/im-pei-1917-2019.pdf

* He was born in 1917 in Guangzhou, China.
* His father is Tsa Yee Pei, a prominent banker.
* He lived to the age 102.
* His family had lived in the cultural center of Suzhou for 500 years.
* As a teenager, the architecture in the cosmopolitan city of Shanghai has impacted his social life.
* He studied in the United States. Initially, he enrolled at the University of Pennsylvania. 
* He transferred to MIT and earned his Bachelor of Architecture in 1940. 
* He also earned his master's degree from Harvard's Graduate School of Design (GSD), studying under Bauhaus giants Walter Gropius and Marcel Breuer.
* His wife is Eileen Woo, a landscape architecture student. They married in 1942.
* He and his wife had four children.
* He joined the real estate development firm Webb and Knapp, run by William Zeckendorf in 1949. It is an unusual step for an architect at the time. 
* In 1960, he built his own architecture firm I.M. Pei & Associates for non-commercial commissions.
* His most significant project is the design of John F. Kennedy Library and Museum in 1964.
* Pei suffered from career setbacks while the John Hancock Tower in Boston, designed by his partner Henry Cobb, suffered from disastrous engineering failures. The blame seriously damaged the firm's reputation.
* Pei became the first foreign architect in modern China in 1978 since the Chinese revolution. He chose to design the Fragrant Hill Hotel. 
* Pei won the Pritzker Prize in 1983.
* Pei used the $100,000 Pritzker prize money to establish a scholarship for Chinese architectural students to study in the U.S.
* Pei’s design for the Louvre pyramid was the most controversial, yet eventually most recognizable and celebrated commission of his career. 
* Pei’s work is considered as a new chapter in modernism that infused it with cross-cultural pluralism and a worldly interpretation. 
* Pei compared the architectural design step to making a fine wine, stating that it requires nurturing with utmost care and patience.
* Pei believed that great architecture should be relevant and engage with the ethics, history, culture, and social politics of its surrounding context.
* Pei successfully advised the Chinese government to implement zoning restrictions to preserve the magnificent views from within Beijing's Forbidden City.
* Pei advised Calvin Tsao, the author of the text, that when considering commissions, it is always better to "choose a curious and spirited client rather than a seductive project."

### Submission
In this bird-and-flower painting, Yin Hong, the Chinese artist who lived in the late 15th century during the Ming Dynasty, illustrates a scene of many different kinds of birds and flowers in a natural setting. The work is rendered on a 7'10 1/2" x 6'5" silk scroll. The artist wisely utilized black ink to render the background objects to provide a heavy theme but brilliantly used bright color to highlight the birds and flowers in this wonderful Chinese painting. 

Black ink is used to provide a solid theme for this elegant work. The background of the work shows brown color, as also observed in most other Chinese ink paintings on silk, due to the use of natural ochre pigments. The artist uses black ink to illustrate a willow tree growing from the ground on the right edge of the work. The heavy trunk of the willow tree leaned toward the left and occupied one third of the background. On the top, its thin branches extend horizontal toward the left. Its slender and flexible branches with narrow leaves hung down over the water on the left portion of the painting, presumably a pound. Water waves were rendered on the left of the painting. The artist used smaller gaps between waves on the upper side to create a sense of three dimensional space, with the waves closer to the viewer having bigger gaps. The trunk has many knots and cavities. The artist rendered the willow truck overlapping in front of several white cherry blossom branches extending from the right. A few leaves were rendered on the branches with several white cherry blossom flowers crisply bringing the sense of spring to the viewers. In the background, the lower portion of three tree trunks with knots and cavities vaguely showing in the center top in the work, without their upper portion and leaves visible to the viewers, creating a sense of mystique in the distance. A big Taihu stone is rendered in the lower portion of the painting. Taihu Stones are often seen in Chinese gardens. Rich people in ancient China usually purchased them as decoration in their garden for their mystical shape. They are famous in China and are limestone with many holes. The ground, Taihu stone, the tree trunk, the waves are all painted with only black ink. This monochromatic style is often observed in many other Chinese paintings. 

Color objects in this artworks grab the viewer’s attention! The artist rendered flowers and birds with vivid bright colors. In the lower portion, we see the green leaves and several big flowers. From the left to the right: a purple camellia, a white chrysanthemum, and a pink chrysanthemum. The viewer can also peek through the hole of the Taihu stone to see a small purple camellia bulb hiding. On the right vertically from the top: A golden chrysanthemum, a white paeonia lactiflora half showing behind the stone, a big red paeonia lactiflora, a smaller pink paeonia lactiflora, and a smaller white magnolia. The artist uses the color of these flowers to grab viewers attention. There are also many birds in the painting. The biggest two are a pair of peacocks. The smaller female one, without an elaborated tail, is closest to the viewer on the lower part of the work, extending its neck eating a small flower. A larger male peacock, rendered in the center of the work, exhibiting his beautiful tail on top of the Taihu Stone, between the white and golden chrysanthemum. A pair of pheasants, one brown, one black and white, stood behind the big willow tree trunk. Both pheasants have red features around their eyesockets. There are many different types of small birds depicted in the painting, some are in black and white, while others are in yellow, green, blue, or red. Some of them are flying, while others are standing on top of the ground or tree branches. The rendering of these animals and vegetation produce a harmonic and peaceful view, reminding us about heaven.

Vivid patterns are showing in several places in this painting. The most obvious one is the eyespot pattern on the peacock tail, and the pattern on the rest of their feathers. On the left, the pattern of curve lines illustrate the water waves. The pendulous willow branches on the top and details of the chrysanthemum petals also form elegant organic patterns. The textures of the willow barks and the surface of the Taihu stone is depicted with monochromatic patterns. 

This late 15 century Chinese painting in a sense is similar to many still life paintings produced in the Netherlands a couple hundred years later. Both focused on the rendering of natural life and documented different kinds of biological species in nature for us to peek into the natural world several hundreds years ago.

### Submission
What are 3 distinguishing architectural features that immediately identify a stupa and a mosque (or Islamic inspired structure) ?
Stupas are large rounded mounds, with square railings on the top and a mast with umbrellalike tiers rising in the center. The rounded mound form represents the curving form of the universe. The square railings represent the Buddhist heavens. The mast mark denotes the axis of the universe. Each stupa usually hosts relics in the center. 

All Muslim mosques have at least one minaret on the outside, a mihrab inside, and heavily decorated with geometry and vegetational pattern.  The minaret is an exterior tall tower for the muezzin to stand in high position to call their believers for prayer. The mihrab is a niche on the qibla wall inside the mosques, to mark the direction of Mecca, to direct the congregations to pray toward. The interior and often the exterior wall of the mosques, based on Muslim theology, were decorated with geometry patterns, vegetational patterns and Arabic calligraphy and lack any shapes of human figure nor animal life forms.

Describe the devotional practice that takes place in a stupa and in a mosque.
Buddhists visit Stupa by entering the toranas, gateway, then circling the building on foot to view the decorated story of Buddhas on the railings. They walk by following the cardinal directions as re-enacting the rotational movement of the celestial bodies around the axis. The believer meditates based on the narratives of Buddhas’s life story carved on the decoration. 

Muslim come to mosques to pray. Most of them will come to the mosque for Friday afternoon prayer but some will come to the five times a day prayer. During their prayer, they take a prostrate position in a grid formation while facing the direction of Mecca.

How does the space accommodate devotional rituals in a stupa and a mosque (or Islamic inspired structure)?
The stupa accommodates the Buddhist devotional rituals from the highly decorated relief, following the circulating form around the stupa. These reliefs depict the life story of Buddhas, and also sometimes the jataka tales, about the story of the previous lives of the Buddha. These reliefs form a continuous narrative and allow the believers to learn their faith by viewing the visual representation. 

All Muslim mosques accommodate their rituals by providing a large space for their followers in prostrate position to pray during their communal prayer meeting. All mosques have a qibla wall, indicated by the mihrab, a niche, to mark the direction of Mecca for the worshipper to face during their prayer. While I visit mosques in Turkey, their mosques often have many washing stations outside on the exterior wall, with seats, to allow worshipers to wash their feet before entering the mosques. This is needed because they need to take off their shoes with clean feet to enter the mosques.

A hallmark of Chinese painting, particularly (but not limited to) that of the literati, is the incorporation of calligraphy into the composition.

### Submission
What does the incorporation of poetry and calligraphy in literati painting tell us about the artist?
Dong Qichang linked the incorporation of calligraphy to literati painting together through the common expression of brushstrokes: “When a scholar turned to painting, he applied to it the methods of writing unusual characters in the grass or clerical script, making his tree resemble curved iron bars and his mountains look as if they were drawing in the sand. … His painting will then have a scholar's breath” (Neave 210). The incorporation of poetry also informs us about how much the artist read and understood ancient Chinese history, literacy, and poetry. The level of their poetic achievement and expression often show us about their level of  literacy. Some of them will write well known pre-existing poems but often time their own creation. Both require a fair amount of study of language art and well rounded history training. 

Why is calligraphy frequently incorporated into artwork in China?
Calligraphy was considered “the highest form of artistic expression in China” for a very long time (“Ming Literati Painters”). Since Chinese painters used brush and ink and depended on the construction of brushstrokes to render their artworks, the expressive language is tightly associated with calligraphy. Another reason is that Chinese painters after the Song dynasty were mainly scholars, not artisans. Literati painters often like to express their artistic emotion of the painting with words, in the form of poetry, or write down their story of such creation, therefore the calligraphy is incorporated into the artworks. Not all the calligraphy on the paintings is poetry. For example, the artist's inscription in Beggars and Street Characters is not a poem, but rather a description of his work (“Ming Professional Painters”). In Chinese tradition, the patron or later owners of the works sometimes also put their colophon in calligraphy next to the painting. For example, Zhang Fengyi and Wen Jia both leave their colophon on the painting Beggars and Street Characters too. Therefore, not all the calligraphy shown on Chinese paintings are from the same original artist. 

How do you think the incorporation of calligraphy and poetry affect the viewers' experience of the art?
Calligraphy, poetry and painting extend the artistic expression into three different dimensions. Painting is a two dimensional art, and often depicts three dimensional space. Poetrary, use words, and sounds to extend the visual image into an intellectual dimension, with story telling and often adding the temporal dimension. Since the poem could be read, it also enhanced the artistic expression into the audio dimension. Calligraphy expresses the poem into an abstract art, since each Chinese ideographic character is an abstract shape and different calligraphic style and their line arrangement is also abstract art, it further extends the expression into a higher view of the artistic expression. Therefore, the combined expression of calligraphy, poetry, and painting together enlarge the dimensionality of the artistic representation into abstraction, audio, and temporal spaces.

Do you think art is a more effective than words?  Why or why not?
The communication of words, either through speech, text, or music lyric, are linear therefore one dimensional. Paintings and photography are two dimensional medium, sculpture, static installation arts, and movies are three dimensional medium. Electronic installation arts, such as Nam June Paik's Electronic Superhighway (“Art of South Kore"), and dance are four dimensional mediums.
